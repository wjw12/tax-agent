# Form 8862 Instructions — 2025 Identity And Recovery Notes

## Source Pointer

- IRS instructions: `Form 8862` for tax year 2025

## 2025 Identity Rules

- Beginning in tax year 2025, `CTC` and `ACTC` require a valid
  employment-authorized `SSN` issued on or before the return due date,
  including extensions.
- On a joint return, one spouse must have a valid `SSN`; the other spouse must
  have either an `SSN` or `ITIN` issued on or before the due date.
- The qualifying child must also have a valid employment-authorized `SSN`
  issued on or before the due date for `CTC` or `ACTC`.
- Children identified only by `ITIN` or `ATIN` do not qualify for `CTC` or
  `ACTC`, but may still be relevant for `ODC`.
- `ODC` and `AOTC` use different taxpayer and dependent `TIN` rules from
  `CTC` and `ACTC`.
- If an `ITIN` or `ATIN` application was made on or before the due date and the
  IRS later issues the number, the instructions treat it as timely issued.

## When Form 8862 Is Required

File `Form 8862` when the taxpayer is reclaiming `EIC`, `CTC`, `ACTC`, `ODC`,
or `AOTC` after a prior reduction or disallowance that was not just a math or
clerical error.

## Exceptions And Ban Rules

- Do not file `Form 8862` if the credit was already re-allowed after a prior
  `Form 8862` filing and has not been disallowed again for a substantive
  reason.
- Do not file it during an active `2`-year reckless-disregard ban or `10`-year
  fraud ban except through the IRS appeal path described in the instructions.
- A ban-period appeal requires the taxpayer to file the later-year return with
  `Form 8862` attached and follow the notice-based challenge process.
