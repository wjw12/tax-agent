# Form 1040-NR — 2025 Delta Rules

Use this file only when the case is on the `Form 1040-NR` path.

## Schedule 1-A Rule

For tax year 2025, `Form 1040-NR` picks up the new `Schedule 1-A` flow.
Do not leave those deductions on older default schedules just because the case
is nonresident.

Nonresident aliens generally cannot claim the qualified passenger vehicle loan
interest deduction.

## Treaty Reporting Rule

For tax year 2025, follow the 2025 `1040-NR` treaty reporting mechanics:

- taxable wages stay on `Form 1040-NR` line `1a`
- treaty-exempt income goes on line `1k`
- treaty detail belongs on `Schedule OI`, item `L`

Do not net treaty-exempt income out of the wage field itself.

If a payer reported gross compensation on `1042-S` or withheld as if the treaty
did not apply, do not rewrite the wage field to a net amount. Keep the gross
income facts intact and report the treaty exemption separately.

## Form 8833 Escalation Rule

Treat any `1040-NR` treaty claim as unsupported when the case appears to
require `Form 8833` and the repo does not have an explicit supported path for
that disclosure.

Do not guess that `Form 8833` is unnecessary just because a treaty benefit is
common. Confirm whether the claim fits an exception documented in the official
case materials or official IRS instructions loaded for the case.

## Hungary 2025 Rule

For returns covering tax year 2025, do not allow a treaty-based exclusion under
the U.S.-Hungary income tax treaty unless the case materials contain an official
source showing a valid 2025 filing position. The treaty termination is a live
rule difference that overrides older model memory.

## Detect-And-Escalate Topics

Detect these 2025 topics early and treat them as unsupported unless the repo
gains explicit form support:

- `Trump accounts` and `Form 4547`
- farmland-sale installment elections and `Form 1062`
- 2025 adoption-credit changes that require `Form 8839`

Do not silently file around these issues.
